The Proposal - August 3, 2013

We had decided in July to have our housewarming party in August

that morning

From the start to the end, it was constant moving (or at least it seemed). Before it started, Keshaun and I were both trying to get everything straightened away with the food and set-up. Before anyone came, Keshaun had asked me to wear this turquoise dress that he likes on me. My response: “No. Why?” LOL. But I didn’t say it rudely (at least, I don’t think so). My reasoning (if I remember correctly): I don’t think I had shaved, and putting on that dress would have required me to do that and take more time. And I had no time for that. LOL. At least he tried, haha.

I awoke to put together the mammoth of a grill we had just purchased from Home Depot. I was nervous about many things but proposing wasn't one of them. I mean yes I was going to be proposing, but that decision was somehow the most clear decision for me to make that day. My mind was on filling the time with ensuring everything was prepared for the housewarming. In hindsight, I was probably a little nervous about the proposal, but I had to fill my time with busywork, and there was plenty of it.

that afternoon

Keshaun pulled me off to the side and said something along the lines of, “Hey, Nick and Verna are leaving. Let’s get everyone together to say thank you.” And I thought that was a good idea. So we pull everyone to the living room, and I’m on the stairs, and my thank yous are unprepared and awkward. “Thanks guys for coming.” LOL. I mean, that was pretty much all I said.

Keshaun went after me, and his was a little bit more prepared (obviously), but then he throws the curve ball by saying something along the lines of “But that’s not my only agenda…”

After the guests arrived, I then realized the weight of the situation. Nobody but I knew what was going down. I was so nervous I could hardly grill the food. As the party progressed, I recall telling my oldest brother and a few others what was going to happen.

Keshaun pops the question

My first reaction, out loud: “Shut up.” I had absolutely no hunch that it would happen today. I was in such a state of shock that I just didn’t know how to act or what to do.

So yeah, my hand was shaking, and I was still in shock. I asked people around me (his family) if they knew about it, and of course, no one knew until that day. It was pretty surreal. The euphoria from it is unbelievable.

As we gave a toast for all that had come to our housewarming, I mentioned that I had one more thing to address. I got down on one knee and asked the question "Jonalyn, will you marry me?" It took some time for her to realize what was happening, but she eventually said yes. It was a great feeling to have everyone around for the event.

    Rixey Manor

    9155 Pleasant Hill Lane | Rixeyville, VA 22737

    Our ceremony and reception will be held at Rixey Manor - the ceremony will be conducted outdoors with dinner and reception to follow inside the ballroom.

    The historical Rixey Manor was built in 1801 by Charles Rixey and remained in the Rixey family for the next 200 years. The history of the family is amazing, and much of the original house is the same as it was through the Civil War.

    The venue will have an open top roof deck with panoramic views of the sunset behind the Blue Ridge mountains and the view from the front of the house overlooks a beautiful lake. Inside the ballroom are nine feet ceilings, chandeliers and floor to ceiling windows.
